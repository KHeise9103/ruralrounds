“I Was the Only One There That Night”

By Anonymous | Posted June 30, 2025

It was 2:43 a.m. when the sheriff's office called. A rollover on Highway 14. I was the only nurse on call for the night at our 6-bed critical access hospital.

The paramedics were en route with a young man in his 20s, hypotensive and unresponsive. No CT. No trauma surgeon. Just me, a freshly trained EMT, and the knowledge that the nearest trauma center was 78 minutes away on dry roads.

This wasn’t the first time I’d stood in a room and made decisions far above my pay grade. It won’t be the last. Rural medicine requires flexibility, intuition, and a whole lot of hope.

This blog is a place to share these moments—big and small—and remind each other that we’re not alone out here.
